# Bible Study: The Fifth Commandment — Honor Father and Mother (Exo 20:12)

## Question
What does the Bible say about honoring parents? This is "the first commandment with promise" (Eph 6:2). What does "honor" (kabed, H3513) mean in Hebrew? Trace this through Proverbs (1:8; 6:20; 23:22), Jesus's defense of the commandment against Corban tradition (Mat 15:3-6; Mark 7:9-13), Paul's application to Gentile Christians (Eph 6:1-3; Col 3:20), and Jesus's own submission to His parents (Luke 2:51). How do Jesus's statements about prioritizing God over family (Mat 10:37; Luke 14:26) relate to this commandment? **Key Findings from Related Studies:**

1. **law-14 (E20/E361):** Jesus explicitly called the 5th Decalogue commandment "the commandment of God" (entolen tou Theou) in both Mat 15:3 and Mrk 7:8,9,13 — defending it as binding against the Corban tradition.

2. **jesus-commandments-nt-vs-ot:** Jesus affirmed ALL TEN commandments. For the 5th: Mat 15:4; 19:19; Mark 7:10; Mark 10:19; Luke 18:20; Eph 6:2.

3. **nt-commandments-vs-ordinances:** The word entole (G1785) distinguishes God's moral commandments from dogma (G1378, ordinances). The 5th commandment is explicitly identified with entole terminology.

### Focus Areas

1. **Hebrew word study of kabed (H3513):** The root meaning "to be heavy/weighty" as the basis for "honor." How does the metaphor of heaviness become honor? Trace the semantic range: heavy → burdensome → glorious → honorable. Compare with kabod (H3519, glory).

2. **The unique promise attached (Eph 6:2-3):** Paul calls this "the first commandment with promise" — what is the significance of the land promise in Exo 20:12 / Deu 5:16, and how does Paul apply it to Gentile Christians?

3. **Leviticus 19:3 parallel — "fear" (yare, H3372) vs. "honor" (kabed, H3513):** Two different Hebrew words used for the parent-child relationship. What does the reversal of order (mother then father in Lev 19:3 vs. father then mother in Exo 20:12) signify?

4. **Corban controversy (Mat 15:3-6; Mrk 7:9-13):** Jesus's strongest defense of a specific Decalogue commandment. How did the Corban tradition nullify the 5th commandment? What principle does Jesus establish about tradition vs. commandment?

5. **Proverbs wisdom tradition on parental instruction:** The extensive wisdom corpus (Pro 1:8; 6:20; 23:22 and many more) that develops the commandment into practical life guidance.

6. **The tension: God over family (Mat 10:37; Luk 14:26):** How does "hate" father and mother (miseo, Luk 14:26) relate to "honor" father and mother? Is this a contradiction, a qualifying hierarchy, or something else?

7. **Jesus's own example (Luk 2:51; Jhn 19:26-27):** Jesus's submission to earthly parents and His care for Mary from the cross.

8. **Penalty provisions (Exo 21:15,17; Lev 20:9; Deu 21:18-21; 27:16):** The severity of OT penalties for dishonoring parents — what do these reveal about the commandment's weight?

9. **NT application scope (Eph 6:1-3; Col 3:20):** Paul's specific adaptation of the commandment to the Christian household. The role of "in the Lord" (Eph 6:1) and "well pleasing unto the Lord" (Col 3:20).

10. **Cross-commandment connections:** This is the bridge commandment between the first table (duties to God, commandments 1-4) and the second table (duties to neighbor, commandments 6-10). How does honoring parents relate to both tables?
