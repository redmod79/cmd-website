### Focus Areas

1. **Creation foundation**: Gen 2:24 establishes the "one flesh" marriage principle before Sinai. How does this pre-Sinai origin affect the commandment's scope? Jesus appeals to Gen 2:24 in Mat 19:4-6 as the creation baseline.

2. **Leviticus 18 comprehensive scope**: The chapter addresses incest (6-18), adultery (20), homosexuality (22), bestiality (23), and states the Canaanites were expelled for these same violations (24-30). This establishes the sexual ethic as universal, not merely Israelite.

3. **Jesus' two-fold deepening**: (a) Adultery extended to lustful intent (Mat 5:27-28); (b) Divorce restricted -- Moses allowed it "for the hardness of your hearts" but "from the beginning it was not so" (Mat 19:8). Jesus appeals to Gen 2:24 and tightens the standard.

4. **The porneia exception clause**: Mat 19:9 -- "except it be for porneia" -- the scope and meaning of porneia (G4202) in this context.

5. **Paul's body-temple theology**: 1 Cor 6:18-20 -- "your body is the temple of the Holy Ghost" -- the theological basis for sexual purity. Also 1 Cor 7:1-16 on marriage, celibacy, and the believing spouse.

6. **Romans 1:26-27**: Paul's treatment of homosexual acts as "against nature" -- how this connects to the Lev 18 framework.

7. **Hebrews 13:4**: "Marriage is honourable...but whoremongers and adulterers God will judge" -- the positive and negative sides.

8. **Eschatological exclusion**: Rev 21:8 and 22:15 -- pornos (fornicators/whoremongers) excluded from the new Jerusalem.

9. **Figurative dimension**: Adultery/whoredom as metaphor for idolatry (Jer 3:2; Ezk 16; Hos 1; Rev 17) -- spiritual unfaithfulness.

10. **Pre-Sinai evidence**: Gen 20:3 (Abimelech warned), Gen 26:10-11 (Abimelech recognized guilt), Gen 38:24 (Judah's sentence on Tamar), Gen 39:9 (Joseph: "sin against God") -- the moral awareness before Sinai.
