# Bible Study: 8th Commandment — Do Not Steal (Exo 20:15)

## Question
What does the Bible say about the eighth commandment? Trace it beyond physical theft to man-stealing/kidnapping (Exo 21:16; Deu 24:7; 1 Tim 1:10), defrauding workers (Lev 19:13; Jas 5:1-6), just weights and measures (Lev 19:35-36; Deu 25:13-15; Amo 8:5), and robbing God (Mal 3:8-10). What does the NT say — Paul (Eph 4:28), the rich young ruler (Mat 19:18), Zacchaeus's restitution (Luk 19:8), and the kingdom exclusion (1 Cor 6:10)? Examine: Exo 20:15, Exo 21:16, Lev 19:11,13,35-36, Deu 24:7; 25:13-15, Mal 3:8-10, Mat 19:18, Luk 19:8, Eph 4:28, 1 Cor 6:10, Jas 5:1-6, 1 Tim 1:10.

### Focus Areas
(Derived from tool discoveries)

1. **The commandment verb ganab (H1589)** — Analyze morphology and semantic range. Ganab implies stealth/secrecy (as opposed to gazal, [H1497](https://www.blueletterbible.org/lexicon/h1497/kjv/wlc/0-1/){:target="_blank"}, which is open robbery/plunder). How does this distinction matter for the commandment's scope?

2. **Man-stealing as capital theft (Exo 21:16; Deu 24:7)** — The Nave's KIDNAPPING entry shows this as a death-penalty offense under the 8th commandment umbrella. 1 Tim 1:10 uses andrapodistes (G405), a NT hapax legomenon. The slavery study already established the connection — verify and integrate.

3. **Defrauding workers (Lev 19:13; Deu 24:14-15; Mal 3:5; Jas 5:4)** — Nave's WAGES and EMPLOYER entries reveal extensive OT regulation. [H6231](https://www.blueletterbible.org/lexicon/h6231/kjv/wlc/0-1/){:target="_blank"} (ashaq, oppress/defraud) appears in Lev 19:13 alongside the 8th commandment context. [G650](https://www.blueletterbible.org/lexicon/g650/kjv/tr/0-1/){:target="_blank"} (apostereo) connects Mrk 10:19 (Jesus adds "defraud not" to the commandment list), 1 Cor 6:7-8, and Jas 5:4.

4. **Just weights and measures (Lev 19:35-36; Deu 25:13-15; Pro 11:1; Amo 8:5)** — Nave's WEIGHTS and BALANCES entries show this as a form of theft by deception. The prophets (Hosea, Amos, Micah) specifically denounce false balances as covenant violation.

5. **Robbing God (Mal 3:8-10)** — [H6906](https://www.blueletterbible.org/lexicon/h6906/kjv/wlc/0-1/){:target="_blank"} (qaba) is used uniquely in Malachi — "Will a man rob God?" Nave's TITHES entry cross-references this. The verb qaba means "to cover/defraud."

6. **Restitution principle (Exo 22:1-4; Lev 6:2-5; Luk 19:8)** — Nave's RESTITUTION entry links OT law to Zacchaeus. The law required 2x-5x restitution; Zacchaeus voluntarily offers 4x. EZK 33:15 — restoring the pledge is a condition for life.

7. **NT deepening and transformation (Eph 4:28)** — Paul does not merely prohibit stealing; he commands the positive opposite: work and give. This mirrors the 6th commandment pattern (don't murder → love). Kingdom exclusion: thieves, extortioners will not inherit (1 Cor 6:10).

8. **Prophetic lists combining commandments** — Jer 7:9-10 and Hos 4:2 list theft alongside murder, adultery, false oaths (Decalogue violations together). This pattern was already documented in cmd-07 and cmd-08.

9. **Jesus and theft** — The rich young ruler encounter (Mat 19:18); Zacchaeus (Luk 19:8); temple cleansing as "den of thieves" (Mat 21:13); the thief on the cross (Luk 23:39-43); Jesus as door vs. thief/robber (Jhn 10:1-10).

10. **Cross-commandment connections** — Stealing intersects with the 9th commandment (dishonesty, false dealing — Lev 19:11 links "steal" and "deal falsely" and "lie"), the 10th commandment (coveting precedes stealing — Jos 7:21 Achan), and the 5th commandment (Pro 28:24 "whoso robbeth his father or mother").
