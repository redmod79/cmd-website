    Strong's Lookups — Raw Output¶

    H3513 — כָּבַד (kabed) — "to be heavy; to honor"¶

    Transliteration: kabad / kabed Pronunciation: kaw-bad Part of Speech: Verb BLB Count: 116 occurrences

    Definition: A primitive root; to be heavy, i.e. in a bad sense (burdensome, severe, dull) or in a good sense (numerous, rich, honorable); causatively, to make weighty (in the same two senses): abounding with, more grievously afflict, boast, be chargeable, be dim, glorify, be (make) glorious (things), glory, (very) great, be grievous, harden, be (make) heavy, be heavier, lay heavily, (bring to, come to, do, get, be had in) honour (self), (be) honourable (man), lade, more be laid, make self many, nobles, prevail, promote (to honour), be rich, be (go) sore, stop.

    Top translations: heavy (8), honourable (3), Honour (3), glorious (3), hardened (2), honour (2+), glorified (2+)

    H3519 — כָּבוֹד (kabod) — "glory, honor, splendor"¶

    Transliteration: kabod Pronunciation: kaw-bode Part of Speech: Masculine noun BLB Count: 200 occurrences

    Definition: From H3513; properly, weight, but only figuratively in a good sense, splendor or copiousness: glorious(-ly), glory, honour(-able).

    Top translations: the glory (40), glory (22), my glory (12), and honour (11), his glory (10), honour (10), thy glory (7)

    H3515 — כָּבֵד (kabed adj.) — "heavy; severe; honorable"¶

    Transliteration: kabed Pronunciation: kaw-bade Part of Speech: Adjective BLB Count: 38 occurrences

    Definition: From H3513; heavy; figuratively in a good sense (numerous) or in a bad sense (severe, difficult, stupid): (so) great, grievous, hard(-ened), (too) heavy(-ier), laden, much, slow, sore, thick.

    Top translations: grievous (7), great (7), heavy (6), sore (4)

    H3514 — כֹּבֶד (kobed) — "weight, heaviness"¶

    Transliteration: kobed Pronunciation: ko-bed Part of Speech: Masculine noun BLB Count: 4 occurrences

    Definition: From H3513; weight, multitude, vehemence: grievousness, heavy, great number.

    Top translations: heavy (2), the grievousness (1), and a great number (1)

    H3372 — יָרֵא (yare) — "to fear, to revere"¶

    Transliteration: yare Pronunciation: yaw-ray Part of Speech: Verb BLB Count: 314 occurrences

    Definition: A primitive root; to fear; morally to revere; causatively to frighten: affright, be (make) afraid, dread(-ful), (put in) fear(-ful, -fully, -ing), (be had in) reverence(-end), see, terrible (act, -ness, thing).

    Top translations: fear (44), afraid (36), Fear (28), feared (19), terrible (13), reverence (2)

    Key usage: Used in Lev 19:3 for the parent-child relationship: "Ye shall fear every man his mother, and his father."

    H3365 — יָקַר (yaqar) — "to be heavy; to be precious"¶

    Transliteration: yaqar Pronunciation: yaw-kar Part of Speech: Verb BLB Count: 11 occurrences

    Definition: A primitive root; properly, apparently, to be heavy, i.e. (figuratively) valuable; causatively, to make rare (figuratively, to inhibit): be (make) precious, be prized, be set by, withdraw.

    Top translations: precious (3), be precious (2), let (2)

    G5091 — τιμάω (timao) — "to prize, to honor"¶

    Transliteration: timao Pronunciation: tim-ah Part of Speech: Verb BLB Count: 21 occurrences

    Definition: From G5092; to prize, i.e. fix a valuation upon; by implication, to revere.

    Top translations: Honour (9), honoureth (2), honour (1), was valued (1), did value (1), honours (1)

    Verses where G5091 = "Honour": Mat 15:4; Mat 19:19; Mar 7:10; Mar 10:19; Luk 18:20; Eph 6:2; 1Ti 5:3; 1Pe 2:17 (x2)

    G5092 — τιμή (time) — "value, honor, price"¶

    Transliteration: time Pronunciation: tee-may Part of Speech: Feminine noun BLB Count: 43 occurrences

    Definition: From G5091; a value, i.e. money paid, or (concretely and collectively) valuables; by analogy, esteem (especially of the highest degree), or the dignity itself: honour, precious, price, some.

    Top translations: honour (29), price (4), with a price (2)

    G5219 — ὑπακούω (hupakouō) — "to hear under; to obey"¶

    Transliteration: hupakouō Pronunciation: hoop-ak-oo Part of Speech: Verb BLB Count: 21 occurrences

    Definition: From hupo (G5259) and akouō (G191); to hear under (as a subordinate), i.e. to listen attentively; by implication, to heed or conform to a command or authority: hearken, be obedient to, obey.

    Top translations: obey (8), obeyed (3), be obedient (1), to hearken (1)

    Verses where G5219 = "obey": Mat 8:27; Mar 4:41; Eph 6:1; Col 3:20; Col 3:22; 2Th 1:8; 2Th 3:14; Heb 5:9

    G5218 — ὑπακοή (hupakoe) — "obedience"¶

    Transliteration: hupakoe Pronunciation: hoop-ak-o-ay Part of Speech: Feminine noun BLB Count: 15 occurrences

    Definition: From G5219; attentive hearkening, i.e. (by implication) compliance or submission: obedience, obey(-ing).

    Top translations: obedience (8), obedient (2), the obedience (2)

    G5043 — τέκνον (teknon) — "a child"¶

    Transliteration: teknon Pronunciation: tek-non Part of Speech: Neuter noun BLB Count: 99 occurrences

    Definition: From the base of G5088; a child (as produced): child, daughter, son.

    Top translations: children (48), son (7), Son (6), the children (6), child (4)

    G1785 — ἐντολή (entole) — "commandment"¶

    Transliteration: entole Pronunciation: en-tol-ay Part of Speech: Feminine noun BLB Count: 71 occurrences

    Definition: From G1781; injunction, i.e. an authoritative prescription: commandment, precept.

    Top translations: commandment (23), commandments (12), precept (2), a commandment (2)

    Key verse: Eph 6:2 — "which is the first commandment with promise"

    G1860 — ἐπαγγελία (epaggelia) — "promise"¶

    Transliteration: epaggelia Pronunciation: ep-ang-el-ee-ah Part of Speech: Feminine noun BLB Count: 53 occurrences

    Definition: From G1861; an announcement (for information, assent or pledge; especially a divine assurance of good): message, promise.

    Top translations: promise (26), promises (11), of promise (6)

    G3404 — μισέω (miseo) — "to hate, detest"¶

    Transliteration: miseo Pronunciation: mis-eh-oh Part of Speech: Verb BLB Count: 42 occurrences

    Definition: From a primary misos (hatred); to detest (especially to persecute); by extension, to love less: hate(-ful).

    Top translations: hate (6), hated (6), shall hate (3), he will hate (2), I hate (2), hating (2)

    Key verse: Luke 14:26 — "If any man come to me, and hate not his father, and mother..." Compare: LXX usage of miseo in Gen 29:31 (Leah "hated" = less loved) and Deu 21:15 (hated wife = less favored)
