    Topics Research¶

    Question¶

    How does love fulfill the law? Jesus summarized the entire Decalogue as love for God and love for neighbor (Mat 22:36-40). Paul declared "love is the fulfilling of the law" (Rom 13:10). John defined love of God as keeping His commandments (1 John 5:3). How do the two great commandments relate to the ten? Does love replace the commandments or does love express itself through commandment-keeping?

    Key Connections Between Topics¶

    1. LOVE and OBEDIENCE are consistently paired -- Exo 20:6 ("them that love me, and keep my commandments") sets the pattern repeated through Deu 7:9, Neh 1:5, Dan 9:4, Jhn 14:15, 1 Jhn 5:3
    2. LOVE and LAW are explicitly linked -- Rom 13:10 ("love is the fulfilling of the law"), Gal 5:14, 1 Tim 1:5 ("end of the commandment is charity"), Jas 2:8 ("royal law")
    3. HOLINESS and LOVE intersect -- Lev 19:2 ("be holy") introduces the chapter containing Lev 19:18 ("love thy neighbour"); 1 Jhn 2:5 ("whoso keepeth his word, in him verily is the love of God perfected")
    4. COMMANDMENTS entry contains Jesus's love-command discourse -- Jhn 13:34,35; 14:11,15,23,24; 15:2,4,5,7-12,14,17 are listed as "precepts of Jesus"
